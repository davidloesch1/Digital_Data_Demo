How Meridian Co. Rethought Onboarding (2021 Case Summary)

Internal research brief · Fictional scenario for research purposes

1 · Context

Meridian Co. is a B2B software firm that found new customers abandoning a long “activation” form mid-way. Before changes, the median time people spent in onboarding was about 12 minutes, and many never returned.

2 · The program and outcomes

Over 14 weeks, a cross-functional team redesigned the flow, capped any single work session at 20 minutes with a gentle “resume later” prompt, and reordered a few high-friction steps.

Completion of the flow moved from 31% to 64% in the third quarter of the year after the redesign; support tickets related to onboarding also dropped, though the brief does not list an exact percent.

3 · What the lead researcher said

According to the write-up, lead researcher Dr. Elias Renn said the single most effective change was not a new color palette or shorter videos—it was splitting the main form across two days so people never faced one marathon block.

4 · Limitations

The case study was limited to North American customers and only organizations with fewer than 200 employees. A follow-up was planned for 18 months later, but that wave was still in the field when this note was published.
